Integer Sequences

In number theory, there are many common sequences which are highly prevalent in nature, such as the Primes or Fibonacci Numbers, with many having intimate ties to more complex fields of science and math, such as cryptography. Yet, many common languages lack built-in support for generating such sequences.

This project seeks to remedy this by offering a standardized API, which is shared across multiple platforms.

The Integer Sequence Library projects are based off the Online Encyclopaedia of Integer Sequences. Unlike some APIs which simply grab the binary file from the OEIS, then serialize them directly into objects, this library, rather than depending on an internet connection and then parsing a text/binary file, allows for native platform support for generating common integer sequences, allowing for much more power and speed as it does away with

  • dependency upon an established internet connection(if no connection or website is down, no functionality)
  • does not need to wait for server responses(sending, receiving and processing http requests takes time)
  • does not need to parse server response into native data (data is already generated natively)
  • does not need to make multiple requests to the server to generate multiple or varying iterations of a sequences, as typically a static series can be generated just once on start-up and used indefinitely while the application runs
  • developers do not need to worry about buffer overflows(receiving too much data from server, blowing the stack) or receiving data which does not support native data-type sizes (either to large or too small of values or the wrong data type entirely)
All of these are major drawbacks and are associated with major overhead, none of which are present when generating native sequences using these libraries.

Ethereum

Ethereum is a decentralized cryptocurrency platform. Developed by Canadian Vitalik Buterin, Ethereum is often referred to as the next generation of the internet, at least as far as electronic currency and E-commerce is concerned.

While most people new to the world of cryptocurrency typically get quickly overwhelmed with the many unfamiliar or foreign concepts, once the realization that, in many ways, the concepts of digital currencies are actually employed in the everyday life of the modern world.

Do you take advantage of Credit cards with points or systems like PC Optimum, or any other customer loyalty systems which rewards users with unique currencies which can't be deemed for legal tender? How about laundry machines that accept reloadable chip-cards, or the wristbands/access passes at amusement parks? Then you are already using cryptocurrencies and don't even know it!

The only difference with Ethereum is the decentralized nature of the system, with the currency exists entirely ephemerally within the distributed blockchain.

Where corporate conglomerates or banking syndicates maintain sole control of the traditional systems their users/consumers access, with Ethereum anyone and everyone can effectively create their own financial system, lending institution, auction house, currency exchange, crowd funding campaign, or entirely secure democratic organizations without ever having to rely on a third party banking institutes or having to be dependant upon payment systems such as PayPal or Amazon, which can chose to shutdown user accounts for no reason, without any notice, effectively halting all future financial transactions.

With Ethereum, power is entirely in the hands of individual users, rather than the faceless multi-national financial institutions or corporations which horde their capital (at the expense of the public), or governments, which would gladly seize private assets to bail out failing business or to serve their own agendas (see Bail-Ins, Civil Forfeiture, Eminent Domain or the Greece financial crisis).

With all transactions being recorded publicly in the blockchain.

Ethereum is truly the future of finance, set to disrupt many traditional institutions through decentralization.
